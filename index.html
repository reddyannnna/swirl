Naruto is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Masashi Kishimoto. It tells the story of Naruto Uzumaki, a young ninja who seeks recognition from his peers and dreams of becoming the Hokage, the leader of his village. The story is told in two parts: the first is set in Naruto's pre-teen years (volumes 1–27), and the second in his teens (volumes 28–72). The series is based on two one-shot manga by Kishimoto: Karakuri (1995), which earned Kishimoto an honorable mention in Shueisha's monthly Hop Step Award the following year, and Naruto (1997).

Demon Slayer

Genre: Dark Fantasy

Story: Tanjiro becomes a demon slayer to save his sister Nezuko and fight demons.

  • Tanjiro

Demon Slayer," also known as Kimetsu no Yaiba, is a popular Japanese manga and anime series. The story follows Tanjiro Kamado, a young boy who becomes a demon slayer after his family is slaughtered and his sister, Nezuko, is turned into a demon. Tanjiro's quest is to avenge his family and find a way to turn Nezuko back into a human.

My Hero Academia

Genre: Superhero

Story: In a world where most people have superpowers (quirks), Izuku Midoriya dreams of becoming the #1 hero.

  • Deku
  • All Might
  • Bakugo

My Hero Academia (Japanese: 僕のヒーローアカデミア, Hepburn: Boku no Hīrō Akademia) is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Kōhei Horikoshi. It was serialized in Shueisha's shōnen manga magazine Weekly Shōnen Jump from July 2014 to August 2024, with its chapters collected in 42 tankōbon volumes. Set in a world where superpowers (called "Quirks") have become commonplace, the story follows Izuku Midoriya, a boy who was born without a Quirk but still dreams of becoming a superhero himself. He is scouted by the world's greatest hero, All Might, who bestows his Quirk to Midoriya after recognizing his potential, and helps to enroll him in a prestigious high school for superheroes in training.
